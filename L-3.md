
#### Laws

   | Law                              | Identity |
|----------------------------------|----------|
| Idempotence of $\vee$            | $p \equiv p \vee p$ |
| Idempotence of $\wedge$          | $p \equiv p \wedge p$ |
| Commutativity of $\vee$          | $p \vee q \equiv q \vee p$ |
| Commutativity of $\wedge$        | $p \wedge q \equiv q \wedge p$ |
| Associativity of $\vee$          | $p \vee (q \vee r) \equiv (p \vee q) \vee r$ |
| Associativity of $\wedge$        | $p \wedge (q \wedge r) \equiv (p \wedge q) \wedge r$ |
| Distributive of $\vee$ over $\wedge$ | $p \vee (q \wedge r) \equiv (p \vee q) \wedge (p \vee r)$ |
| Distributive of $\wedge$ over $\vee$ | $p \wedge (q \vee r) \equiv (p \wedge q) \vee (p \wedge r)$ |
| Identity Law (for $\vee$)        | $p \vee F \equiv p$ |
| Identity Law (for $\wedge$)      | $p \wedge T \equiv p$ |
| Domination Law (for $\vee$)      | $p \vee T \equiv T$ |
| Domination Law (for $\wedge$)    | $p \wedge F \equiv F$ |
| Double Negation                  | $\neg(\neg p) \equiv p$ |
|Tautology Law       | $p\vee \sim q \equiv Tautology$ |
|  Contradiction Law   | $p \wedge \sim p \equiv Contradicition$ |
| De Morgan’s Law                  | $\neg(p \vee q) \equiv \neg p \wedge \neg q$ |
| De Morgan’s Law                  | $\neg(p \wedge q) \equiv \neg p \vee \neg q$ |
| Absorption Law                   | $p \vee (p \wedge q) \equiv p$ |
| Absorption Law                   | $p \wedge (p \vee q) \equiv p$ |
| Implication                      | $p \rightarrow q \equiv \neg p \vee q$ |
| Contrapositive                  | $p \rightarrow q \equiv \neg q \rightarrow \neg p$ |
| Biconditional                    | $p \leftrightarrow q \equiv (p \rightarrow q) \wedge (q \rightarrow p)$ |
| Negation of Implication          | $\neg(p \rightarrow q) \equiv p \wedge \neg q$ |

#### Disjuctive Normal Form (dnf)

   A statement form which consist of Disjunction between Conjunction is called DNF.

   e.g. $(p\wedge q) q \vee(\sim q \wedge p)$

---

#### Conjunction Normal Form (cnf)

   A statement form which consist of conjunction between disjunction is called CNF.
   e.g. $(p \vee q ) \wedge (\sim p \vee q ) \wedge (\sim q)$
